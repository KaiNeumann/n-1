## L-Arginine and Blood Pressure

**L-arginine** is an amino acid that plays a critical role in producing **nitric oxide**, which can help relax blood vessels and potentially reduce blood pressure. The Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1998 acknowledged research on nitric oxide's role in cardiovascular health.

### Dosage
Effective dosages of L-arginine generally range from:
- **Cardiovascular health**: 2 to 6 grams per day
- **Athletic performance**: 3 to 6 grams per day
- **Erectile dysfunction**: 5 grams per day
- **Hypertension**: 6 to 12 grams per day

### Foods Rich in Arginine
Foods high in L-arginine include:
- **Pumpkin and squash seeds**: 5.43g and 4.63g per 100g
- **Dark chocolate**: 3.36g
- **Nuts**: 2.5g
- **Meat**: 1.5 to 2.5g
- **Fish**: 1.5g
- **Legumes**: 0.5 to 1.5g
- **Dairy products**: 0.3 to 0.8g

Incorporating these foods into your diet can help ensure adequate arginine intake.
